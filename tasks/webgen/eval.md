## **Scoring Scale**

**5 — Excellent**

* Nearly all criteria are met
* Only minor or rare issues
* User experience is smooth, predictable, and effective

**4 — Good**

* Most criteria are met
* Some moderate issues but do not block normal use
* Overall positive experience

**3 — Fair**

* Noticeable issues that affect quality but not core use
* Inconsistent experience
* Needs improvement to meet best practices

**2 — Poor**

* Major issues that significantly disrupt usability or functionality
* Users may struggle to complete tasks reliably

**1 — Very Poor**

* Severe failures
* Critical barriers prevent normal use
* Does not meet essential expectations

# Usability Guidelines

## **1. Visibility of System Status**

The design should always keep users informed about what is going on through timely and clear feedback.

## **2. Match Between the System and the Real World**

The design should use the users’ language — not internal jargon — and follow real-world conventions.

## **3. User Control and Freedom**

Users need easy ways to undo actions, exit processes, or recover from mistakes.

## **4. Consistency and Standards**

Users shouldn’t have to question whether different elements mean the same thing. Maintain internal and external consistency.

## **5. Error Prevention**

Prevent errors before they happen by removing error-prone conditions or providing confirmations.

## **6. Recognition Rather than Recall**

Reduce memory load by making actions and information visible instead of requiring users to remember them.

## **7. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use**

Support both novice and expert users by allowing shortcuts and personalization.

## **8. Aesthetic and Minimalist Design**

Interfaces should display only relevant information. Extra content competes with essential content.

## **9. Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors**

Error messages should be clear, human-readable, and provide actionable solutions.

## **10. Help and Documentation**

Provide accessible help when needed — even if the system is designed to be intuitive.
